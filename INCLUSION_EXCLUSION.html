Inclusion-exclusion principle

In combinatorics the inclusion exclusion principle is a couting technique which allows you to determine the cardinality of the union of two finite sets. For two sets the inclusion-exclusion principle states: $$ | A \cup B | = |A| + |B| - | A \cup B | $$ Where $A$ and $B$ are sets and the vertical lines tell us that we are taking the cardinality of these sets. The cardinality is the number of elements in the set.

Syllabus Aims

  • You should be able to state the inclusion exclusion principle.
  • You should be able to explain the derivation of the inclusion exclusion principle using Venn diagrams.
  • You should be able to explain how the inclusion exclusion principle can be used to calculate probabilities such as $P(X=1 \vee Y=1)$
