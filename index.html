Our Philosophy

Mathematics is a language which uses a hierarchy of abstract objects to express meaning. In spite of this, however, we often teach, learn and examine mathematics as if it is simply a set of procedures for solving particular problems each of which needs to be memorised. This is problematic as when students learn this way they often have little conceptual understanding of what they are doing. Consequently, they are unprepared when they comence any research project. At the same time this lack of understanding increases the work load for teachers as they are required to cover the same material over and over again. This website focusses on trying to resolve these problems through the way topics are organized within it. We have divided the material into fine grained topics and have used the sets of topics and the order in which they are taught in each given module to generate a graph that connects all these various topics. Our hope in doing this is two fold. First of all we would like to ensure that students have access to the best materials for learning each topic. Clearly, any material they had from a first year course on differential equations in which solving these was the focus of the module will be better than the sort of review notes that one might give out at the start of the third year course. Our second aim, however, is to show the students how the various topics are connected up as conceptual learning can only begin once students understand how each topic they have studied fits together to form a coherent whole.
