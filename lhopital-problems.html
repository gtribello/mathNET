Solving limits using l'Hopital's Rule : Exercises

Introduction

Remember l'Hopital's rule states that: $$ \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \rightarrow a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} $$ which can be proved by performing a Taylor series expansion of the function in the numerator and the denominator and truncating the expansion at first order.

Exercise 1

Use l'Hopital's rule to find values for the following limits $$ \lim_{x\rightarrow 1 } \frac{ 1 - x^n }{1 - x^m} \qquad \lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \frac{ 1 - e^x }{ x - x^2} \qquad \lim_{x\rightarrow 0} \frac{\sin(3x^2) }{1-\exp(x^2)} \qquad \lim_{x\rightarrow 1} \frac{ \ln(x) }{ x^3 - 1} $$
