The matrix inverse

The inverse of a square matrix $\mathbf{A}$ (if it exists) is the matrix $\mathbf{A}^{-1}$ which satisfies: $$ \mathbf{A}^{-1}\mathbf{A}=\mathbf{A} \mathbf{A}^{-1}=\mathbf{I} $$ where $\mathbf{I}$ is the unit matrix: $$ \mathbf{I} = \left( \begin{matrix} 1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & \cdots & 0 \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ 0 & 0 & \cdots & 1 \\ \end{matrix} \right) $$

Syllabus Aims

  • You should be able to invert $2x2$ and $3x3$ matrices using pen and paper.
  • You should be able write python programs that use libraries to calculate the inverse of a square matrix
  • You should be able to explain how the matrix inverse is used to solve systems of linear equations.
