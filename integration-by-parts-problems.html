Integration by parts : Exercises

Introduction

Remember when solving the following problem that the method of integration by parts is derived by integrating the product rule rule and rearranging the resulting expression. We thus have: $$ \int u \frac{\textrm{d}v}{\textrm{d}x} \textrm{d}x = uv - \int v \frac{\textrm{d}u}{\textrm{d}x} \textrm{d}x $$ When using this method remember that you must divide the function you wish to integrate into a part $u$ and a part $\frac{\textrm{d}v}{\textrm{d}x}$. You then differentiate $u$ and integrate $\frac{\textrm{d}v}{\textrm{d}x}$ and insert what emerges into the equations above. Generally, when you pick the functions to use for $u$ and $\frac{\textrm{d}v}{\textrm{d}x}$ you want to choose functions so that $v \frac{\textrm{d}u}{\textrm{d}x} \textrm{d}x$ is a simpler function to integrate than the original $u \frac{\textrm{d}v}{\textrm{d}x} \textrm{d}x$.
