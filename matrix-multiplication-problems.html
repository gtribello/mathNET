Matrix Multiplication : Exercises

Introduction

Matrices are enormously important in advanced mathematics and these mathematical objects appear in a range of different fields. It is thus important that you understand how to add and multiply matrices. The exercises on this page should consolidate your ability to multiply matrices.

Example problems

Click on the problems to reveal the solution

Problem 1

Calculate the following product of matrices $$ \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{matrix} \right) $$

The product of the two matrices is given by: $$ \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{matrix} \right) = \left( \begin{matrix} ae + bg & af + bh \\ ce + dg & cf + dh \end{matrix} \right) $$

Problem 2

Only one of the following matrix multiplications can be performed. Can you explain which one it is: $$ \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} e \\ f \end{matrix} \right) \qquad \qquad \qquad \left( \begin{matrix} e \\ f \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) $$

When multiplying rectangular matrices the number of columns in the first matrix must be equal to the number of rows in the second matrix. Consequently the following matrix multiplication is possible: $$ \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} e \\ f \end{matrix} \right) = \left( \begin{matrix} ae + bf \\ ce + df \end{matrix} \right) $$ But the following matrix multiplication cannot be performed: $$ \left( \begin{matrix} e \\ f \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) $$

Adding the transpose

If a matrix has a small superscript T above it that means I would like you to transpose the matrix before doing the matrix multiplication. If by contrast you have a matrix product such as $(\mathbf{AB})^T$ that means I would like you to transpose the matrix you get from the mutltiplication.
$$ \left( \begin{matrix} 6 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 \end{matrix} \right)^T \left( \begin{matrix} 3 & 2 \\ -4 & 8 \end{matrix} \right) $$ $$ \left( \begin{matrix} 6 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} 3 & 2 \\ -4 & 8 \end{matrix} \right)^T $$ $$ \left( \begin{matrix} 6 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 \end{matrix} \right)^T \left( \begin{matrix} 3 & 2 \\ -4 & 8 \end{matrix} \right)^T $$ $$ \left( \begin{matrix} 3 & 2 \\ -4 & 8 \end{matrix} \right)^T \left( \begin{matrix} 6 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 \end{matrix} \right)^T $$ $$ \left[ \left( \begin{matrix} 6 & 3 \\ 2 & -1 \end{matrix} \right) \left( \begin{matrix} 3 & 2 \\ -4 & 8 \end{matrix} \right) \right]^T $$
Given the results you obtained in the above exercises, which of the following equalities is correct for any pair of matrices $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$. $$ \mathbf{A}^T \mathbf{B} = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{B} \qquad (\mathbf{A}\mathbf{B})^T = \mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{B}^T \qquad (\mathbf{A}\mathbf{B})^T = \mathbf{B}^T \mathbf{A}^T \qquad (\mathbf{A}\mathbf{B})^T = \mathbf{B} \mathbf{A}^T $$
