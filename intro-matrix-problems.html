Basic matrix problems : Exercises

Introduction

The following problems allow you to practise adding matrices together and multiplying matrices by a scalar.

Worked examples

Click on the problems to reveal the solution

Problem 1

Calculate sum of the following two matrices: $$ \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) + \left( \begin{matrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{matrix} \right) $$

When we add together two matrices we add together the elements term by term as shown here: \begin{equation} \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) + \left( \begin{matrix} e & f \\ g & h \end{matrix} \right) = \left( \begin{matrix} a + e & b + f \\ c + g & d + h \end{matrix} \right) \end{equation}

Problem 2

Calculate the following product of a matrix and a scalar, $\alpha$: $$ \alpha \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) $$

When a matrix is multiplied by a scalar the scalar multiplies each of the elements of the matrix in turn as shown here: \begin{equation} \alpha \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) = \left( \begin{matrix} \alpha a & \alpha b \\ \alpha c & \alpha d \end{matrix} \right) \end{equation}
