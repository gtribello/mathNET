Matrix inverse problems : Exercises

Introduction

The inverse, $\mathbf{A}^{-1}$, of a square matrix, $\mathbf{A}$, is the matrix for which: $$ \mathbf{A}^{-1} \mathbf{A} = \mathbf{A}\mathbf{A}^{-1} = \mathbf{I} $$ where $\mathbf{I}$ is the identity matrix, which has ones for each of its diagonal elements and which has zero for all the off diagonal elements. The methods for calculating the inverse of $2 \times 2$ matrices and $3 \times 3$ matrices are explained in the two worked examples below.

Problem 1

Calculate the inverse of the $2 \times 2$ matrix: $$ \mathbf{A} = \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) $$

We calculate the inverse of the following 2 x 2 matrix: $$ \mathbf{A} = \left( \begin{matrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{matrix} \right) $$ As follows. First compute the determinant of $\mathbf{A}$ as follows: $$ \det(\mathbf{A}) = ad - cb $$ If the determinant is non-zero the inverse exists. You can thus compute the inverse as: $$ \mathbf{A}^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(\mathbf{A})} \left( \begin{matrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{matrix} \right) $$

Problem 2

Compute the inverse of the following $3 \times 3$ matrix $$ \mathbf{M} = \left( \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{matrix} \right) $$

To compute the inverse of a the 3 x 3 matrix shown here follow the steps below. \begin{equation} \mathbf{M} = \left( \begin{matrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{matrix} \right) \end{equation}
  • Step 1: Compute the determinant The determinant of $\mathbf{A}$ is eequal to: $$ \det(M) = (ei - hf) - (di - gf) + (dh-ge) $$ Much as in the two by two cases if the determinant is zero then the matrix is not invertable.
  • Step 2: Transpose the matrix $$ \mathbf{M}^T = \left( \begin{matrix} a & d & g \\ b & e & h \\ c & f & i \end{matrix} \right) $$
  • Step 3: Calculate the adjoint matrix by coputing the cofactors $$ \textrm{adj}(\mathbf{M}) = \left( \begin{matrix} (ei - fh) & -(bi-ch) & (bf - ce) \\ -(di - fg) & (ai - cg) & -(af-cd) \\ (dh - eg) & -(ah - bg) & (ae-bd) \end{matrix} \right) $$
  • Step 4: Multiply the adjoint matrix by the 1 over the determinant $$ \mathbf{M}^{-1} = \frac{1}{\det(\mathbf{M}} \left( \begin{matrix} (ei - fh) & -(bi-ch) & (bf - ce) \\ -(di - fg) & (ai - cg) & -(af-cd) \\ (dh - eg) & -(ah - bg) & (ae-bd) \end{matrix} \right) $$
